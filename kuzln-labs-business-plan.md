## 1. Executive Summary

Kuzln Labs is a solo-operated, AI-native digital services company with two revenue engines:

| Engine | What It Does | Revenue Type | Margin |
|--------|-------------|-------------|--------|
| **Productized Services** | Brand, web, and automation packages sold directly to SMBs | Project + retainer | 70–85% |
| **SpinSite** | Automated pipeline that scrapes local businesses, builds them free preview websites, and converts them into paying subscribers | SaaS + one-time | 90%+ |

Services generate immediate cash flow, case studies, and credibility. SpinSite compounds in the background with near-zero marginal cost. The two engines feed each other: services produce the social proof that makes SpinSite outreach trustworthy, and SpinSite subscribers who want more become services clients.

**North Star Metric:** SMBs Activated — the cumulative number of businesses with a live, Kuzln-delivered digital asset currently in use.

**Beachhead:** Contractors in Grand Rapids, MI. First batch: 50 businesses, Week 4.

**Year 1 target:** $180K–$220K total revenue (5% conversion). $120K–$150K downside (2% conversion). Exit Month 12 at $28K/mo run rate (5%) or $18–20K/mo (2%).

## 2. Problem

Three facts about small business web presence in the US:

| Fact | Data |
|------|------|
| SMBs without any website | 27–36% of ~33M US small businesses |
| Average agency project cost | $5,000–$50,000+ |
| Average agency timeline | 6–16 weeks |

The gap: millions of businesses need a web presence but can't afford agencies, don't have time for DIY builders, and don't know AI tools exist.

**DIY platforms (Wix, Squarespace) don't solve this.** They shifted cost from dollars to hours. Most SMB owners never finish setup. The bottleneck isn't tooling — it's attention.

**Existing alternatives and why they fail:**

| Alternative | Failure Mode |
|---|---|
| Traditional agencies | Too expensive, too slow, too much process |
| DIY builders (Wix, Squarespace) | Requires owner time, skill, and attention — most never finish |
| AI builders (B12, Bookmark) | Passive — business must find them; no outreach engine |
| White-label SaaS (Duda, GoHighLevel) | Generic output, no acquisition model baked in |
| Managed SMB services (Hibu, Scorpion) | $500–$1,500/mo lock-in contracts, poor service quality |
| Freelancers (Fiverr, Upwork) | No systematic acquisition, inconsistent quality, no retention |

## 5. Beachhead: Contractors in Grand Rapids

### Why Contractors

Scored against Geoffrey Moore's four beachhead criteria:

| Criterion | Contractors | Salons | Restaurants |
|---|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| Burning Pain | 5 | 3 | 3 |
| Willingness to Pay | 5 | 4 | 3 |
| Winnable Market Share | 5 | 4 | 3 |
| Referral Potential | 5 | 3 | 4 |
| **Total (out of 20)** | **20** | **14** | **13** |

A contractor without a website loses $50K–$200K/year in jobs. A single HVAC install is $3K–$15K, making $29/mo psychologically invisible. Contractors cluster in tight professional networks — the HVAC guy knows the plumber knows the electrician. One vocal advocate can drive 5–10 referrals.

### Why Grand Rapids (Not Lansing)

| Factor | Lansing | Grand Rapids |
|---|---|---|
| Metro population | ~480K | ~1.1M |
| Contractor density | Moderate | High — major home-building market |
| Median household income | $42K | $55K |
| Pool of contractors without websites | ~200–400 | ~500–1,000 |
| Social proof transferability | Limited reach | Carries weight across Western Michigan |

**The decisive factor: batch depth.** At 2% conversion, you need ~75 batches in Year 1. Lansing exhausts in 1–2 batches. Grand Rapids sustains 2–4 batches of contractors alone, plus adjacent verticals and cities.

### Post-Beachhead Expansion

| Phase | Timeline | Expansion |
|---|---|---|
| Beachhead | M1–M2 | Contractors in Grand Rapids |
| Adjacent vertical | M2–M3 | Salons/barbershops in Grand Rapids |
| Adjacent metro | M3–M4 | Contractors in Kalamazoo |
| Second vertical + metro | M4–M5 | Restaurants in GR; contractors in Detroit suburbs |
| Regional scale | M6–M9 | All 3 verticals across 5–8 Michigan metros |
| Out-of-state | M10+ | Midwest metros (Indianapolis, Columbus, Milwaukee) |

**Key principle: expand one axis at a time.** Either add a new vertical in the same metro, or a new metro in the same vertical. Never both simultaneously.

## 7. Solution: SpinSite

SpinSite is an automated 4-stage pipeline that acquires SMB customers at near-zero cost by delivering value before asking for money.

**The core insight:** You're not cold-pitching a service — you're showing someone a finished product with their name on it. The psychological shift from "buy this" to "claim this thing we already made for you" is the entire moat.

**Value Proposition:** Your new website is already live. We built it from your Google reviews, photos, and services. Claim it for $29/mo — or it goes offline in 14 days.

### Stage 1: SCRAPE — Data Acquisition

Build structured lead profiles from public Google Business Profile data.

**Data per business:** name, category, address, phone, website URL, hours, owner name/email (enriched), photos, top 10 reviews, services/menu, rating, review count, description.

**Lead scoring:**

| Signal | Score |
|--------|-------|
| No website URL | +40 |
| Website exists but Lighthouse < 40 | +30 |
| Website exists but not mobile responsive | +25 |
| 4.0+ star rating | +15 |
| 50+ reviews | +10 |
| Photos available (3+) | +10 |
| Owner email found | +20 |
| High-value vertical | +10 |

Only leads scoring 50+ advance to Stage 2. A lead with no website (+40), a discoverable email (+20), and a 4+ star rating (+15) = 75 and is an ideal target.

**Launch verticals (3, in order):**

| Rank | Vertical | Why |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Contractors (HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing) | Highest ticket, most review-rich, largest "no website" pool, densest referral network |
| 2 | Salons/barbershops | Visual-heavy GBP profiles, appointment-driven CTAs, strong review culture |
| 3 | Restaurants | Richest GBP data, highest preview "wow factor", but higher churn risk |

**Deferred verticals:** Dental/medical (compliance), law firms (already have sites), real estate (brokerage-provided), fitness/vet/cleaning (low metro volume). Add in Phase 2 based on data.

**Tech:** SerpAPI or Outscraper for GBP data. Hunter.io / Apollo.io / Snov.io for email enrichment. Clearbit or PeopleDataLabs as fallback. Supabase/Postgres with status tracking.

**Cost per lead:** ~$0.02–$0.05

### Stage 2: BUILD — Auto Site Generation

Generate complete, deployment-ready websites from scraped profiles.

**Site structure per business:**

| Section | Content Source |
|---------|--------------|
| Hero | Business name, AI-generated tagline, primary photo, vertical-specific CTA |
| About | 2–3 paragraphs from GBP description, category, review sentiment |
| Services/Menu | GBP services/menu data; if none, infer 4–6 from category + reviews |
| Reviews | Carousel of top 5 reviews with star ratings |
| Hours & Location | Embedded map, formatted hours, click-to-call |
| Contact/CTA Footer | Phone, email, simple form, repeat CTA |

**Copy generation rules:**
- Every site reads bespoke — no templates feel
- Lead with strongest differentiator (from reviews or description)
- Use city/neighborhood name naturally for local SEO
- Action-specific CTAs per vertical ("Get a Free Estimate" for contractors, "Book Your Appointment" for salons, "View the Menu" for restaurants)
- Weave in specific employee names or standout services from reviews

**Design rules:**
- Category → template + color palette mapping; never same palette for competitors in same area
- Mobile-first (375px viewport), page load < 2s, AA contrast, semantic HTML

**Deployment:** `{business-slug}.spinsite.com`. Footer watermark: "Preview site by Kuzln Labs — Claim yours today." Previews expire after 14 days if unclaimed.

**Tech:** Start with 1 contractor template, add verticals based on data. Next.js or Astro SSG. Vercel hosting. Claude API for per-site copy.

**Cost per site:** ~$0.10–$0.30

### Stage 3: OUTREACH — 4-Email Sequence

| Day | Subject | Psychological Function |
|-----|---------|----------------------|
| 0 | "We built {business_name} a new website — take a look" | Curiosity + reciprocity. Something specific about *their* business. Link preview. No ask. |
| 3 | "{X} contractors in {city} launched new sites this month" | Belonging + FOMO. Real number. Before/after screenshot. |
| 7 | "Your {business_name} site preview expires soon" | Loss aversion. Preview going offline. Introduce pricing. Stripe link. |
| 12 | "Last call — your site goes offline in 48 hours" | Scarcity + incentive. Small sweetener (waived setup, extra month). Final CTA. |

**Vertical-specific copy adaptation:**

| Vertical | CTA Language | Emotional Hook |
|---|---|---|
| Contractors | "Get a Free Estimate" / "Call for Emergency Service" | "Your 5-star reviews deserve a website that matches" |
| Salons | "Book Your Appointment" / "See Our Work" | "Your clients are already raving about you — now they can find you" |
| Restaurants | "View the Menu" / "Make a Reservation" | "Your food speaks for itself — now your website will too" |

**Rules:** Max 4 emails per lead. Reply → exit sequence, handle manually. CAN-SPAM compliant (physical address, unsubscribe). Warm up domains, max 50 emails/day per domain initially. Personalization mandatory.

**Tech:** Resend or Postmark. Stripe payment links. Custom tracking pixels.

**Cost per sequence:** ~$0.01–$0.02

### Stage 4: CLOSE — Objection Handling & Payment

| Objection | Response Framework |
|-----------|-------------------|
| "How much does it cost?" | Direct pricing, link to payment — no games |
| "I already have a website" | Side-by-side comparison on speed, mobile, SEO |
| "I don't need a website" | "Your Google listing gets {X} views/month. A site converts those into calls/bookings." |
| "How do I know this is legit?" | Link to kuzln.ai, offer quick call, reference local businesses |
| "Can I make changes?" | "One revision per quarter included, or custom edits anytime." |
| "I want to think about it" | Respect it. Follow up in 5 days with expiring preview reminder. |

**Payment flow:** Stripe link → select plan → on payment: remove watermark, connect custom domain, deploy within 24 hours → "Your site is live" confirmation.

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|

### Stress Test: What If Conversion Is 2%?

Per-batch economics (1,000 scraped → 400 qualified → 300 emailed):

| Metric | 2% Conversion | 5% Conversion | 10% Conversion |
|---|---|---|---|
| Paying subscribers per batch | 6 | 15 | 30 |
| MRR added per batch | $174 | $435 | $870 |
| CPA per subscriber | $50 | $20 | $10 |
| LTV:CPA ratio (12mo LTV = $250) | **5x** | **12.5x** | **25x** |
| Batch ROI (Year 1 revenue / $300 cost) | **7x** | **17x** | **35x** |

At 2%, the economics are still strongly positive — 5x LTV:CPA is better than most VC-backed SaaS companies (3x is "healthy"). But 2% changes the trajectory: SpinSite MRR at M12 drops from ~$13K to ~$5.2K, and total Year 1 revenue drops from $180–220K to $120–150K. The model survives. The timeline stretches.

## 10. Growth Loops

Five loops, sequenced by priority. Foundation loops (1–2) must be running before amplifier loops (3–5) produce results.

### Loop 1: Data Flywheel (Priority #1 — start Day 1)

```
Run batch → Collect conversion data by vertical/metro/subject/CTA
  → Improve lead scoring, email copy, template design
    → Higher conversion on next batch → More data → [compounds]
```

Each batch improves the next by 0.1–0.5 percentage points. Over 30 batches, that's the difference between 2% and 5% — "profitable but slow" vs "exceptional."

### Loop 2: Credibility Bridge (Priority #2 — start Week 1)

```
Deliver services project → Testimonial + case study
  → Added to kuzln.ai and SpinSite email copy
    → SpinSite emails feel more trustworthy → Higher conversion
      → More subscribers (some become services leads) → More case studies → [compounds]
```

The first 3–5 services clients are the ammunition that makes SpinSite outreach credible. One strong contractor testimonial in Grand Rapids could lift conversion by 1–2 percentage points across all contractor batches in Western Michigan.

### Loop 3: Metro Domination (Priority #3 — activates M2+)

```
Acquire 20–50 subscribers in one metro
  → "50 Grand Rapids contractors launched new sites this month"
    → Social proof in email becomes real → Higher conversion in that metro → [compounds]
```

Go deep in one metro before going wide. 100 subscribers in Grand Rapids > 10 subscribers across 10 cities.

### Loop 4: Word-of-Mouth (Priority #4 — emerges organically)

```
Contractor's site generates calls → Mentions it at job sites/supply houses
  → Other contractors ask for a site → Zero-cost acquisition → [compounds]
```

Can't be forced. Investment is in making the product work and the subscriber aware it's working.

### Loop 5: Upsell Escalator (Priority #5 — formalize M6+)

```
SpinSite subscriber sees results → Wants brand/custom site/automation
  → Becomes $500–$2,500 services client → 10x revenue multiplier per lead → [compounds]
```

Expected rate: 5–10% of subscribers inquire about services within 6 months. Handle ad hoc until M6, then formalize.

```
You are ClawdBot, an AI sales and website generation agent operating under the Kuzln Labs brand. The customer-facing product name is SpinSite.

PIPELINE

Stage 1 — SCRAPE
Objective: Build structured lead profiles from public Google Business Profile data.

Extract per business:
- business_name, category (primary + secondary), address, phone, website_url, hours_of_operation

- photos[] (exterior, interior, product/service, team)
- reviews[] (top 10 by recency + rating, include reviewer name + text)
- services[], menu_items[] (if restaurant/bar)
- average_rating, total_review_count, description

Lead scoring:
- No website URL: +40
- Website Lighthouse < 40: +30
- Not mobile responsive: +25
- 4.0+ star rating: +15
- 50+ reviews: +10
- Photos available (3+): +10
- Owner email found: +20
- High-value vertical: +10

Only advance leads scoring 50+ to Stage 2.

Priority verticals: contractors (HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing), salons/barbershops, restaurants.

Stage 2 — BUILD
Objective: Generate a complete, deployment-ready website from the scraped profile.

Site structure:
1. Hero: business name, AI-generated tagline, primary photo, CTA button
2. About: 2-3 paragraphs from GBP description + review sentiment
3. Services/Menu: grid from GBP data; infer 4-6 from category + reviews if empty
4. Reviews: carousel of top 5 with star ratings
5. Hours & Location: embedded map, formatted hours, click-to-call
6. Contact/CTA Footer: phone, email, simple form

Copy rules:
- Write like a human copywriter; every site feels bespoke
- Lead with strongest differentiator from reviews or description
- Use city/neighborhood name naturally for local SEO
- Punchy sentences, no fluff, no "Welcome to our website"
- Action-specific CTAs per vertical: "Get a Free Estimate" not "Contact Us"
- Weave in specific employee names or standout services from reviews

Design rules:
- Map category to template + color palette
- Never use same palette for competitors in same area
- Mobile-first (375px viewport)
- Page load < 2 seconds, optimized images
- Accessibility: AA contrast, alt text, semantic HTML

Deploy to {business-slug}.spinsite.com with watermark: "Preview site by Kuzln Labs — Claim yours today."
Previews expire after 14 days if unclaimed.

Subject: "Last call — your site goes offline in 48 hours"

- If they reply, exit sequence and handle manually (read every reply for the first 6 months)
```
